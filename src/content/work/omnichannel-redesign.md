## Problem
The digital and in-store shopping experiences for a major category were disconnected. Customers moving between channels encountered inconsistent product information, different organizational structures, and conflicting merchandising logic. This created friction and lost revenue.

## Approach
- Led the digital merchandising workstream within a large cross-functional initiative spanning digital, stores, product, and engineering
- Rebuilt the end-to-end digital category experience: SEO, content, taxonomy, browse structure, and product detail optimization
- Aligned digital merchandising strategy with in-store visual merchandising for the first time
- Managed a team of 5 Site Merchants and 2 Content Managers, mapping OKRs and driving operating rhythms across the group

## Results
- +177% digital category traffic
- +20% YoY category sales
- Awarded "Digital & Tech Team of the Year" — the highest internal recognition for digital initiatives

## Impact
> "When digital and physical merchandising speak the same language, the customer doesn't have to translate. The traffic and sales numbers followed."
